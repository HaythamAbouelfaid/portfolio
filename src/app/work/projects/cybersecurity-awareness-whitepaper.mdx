## Overview

This project began as a **standard ENG 111 research assignment** and evolved into a **professional cybersecurity awareness and policy proposal** focused on protecting college students from modern cyber threats.

The final deliverable is a **researched open letter** addressed to the **NOVA IT Department and Office of Student Affairs**, advocating for institutional cybersecurity changes based on peer-reviewed research and real-world threat trends.

Rather than treating the assignment as purely academic, I approached it as a **cybersecurity research case study** centered on student safety, digital literacy, and long-term risk reduction.

---

## Problem Statement

College students are among the **most targeted demographics** for cybercrime due to:

- Weak or reused passwords  
- Poor phishing detection skills  
- Unsafe public Wi-Fi usage  
- High reliance on online academic and financial platforms  
- Lack of required cybersecurity education  

These vulnerabilities frequently lead to:

- Identity theft  
- Financial fraud  
- Compromised academic accounts  
- Long-term credit and financial aid damage  

Research shows that many students **overestimate their technical ability** while still failing to recognize basic cyber threats (Hobbs).

---

## Research & Evidence

This project is supported by **credible academic and industry sources**, including:

- **Armas et al.** — *Building a Cybersecurity Culture in Higher Education*  
  - Demonstrates how early, required cybersecurity education significantly reduces campus-wide incidents.
- **Hobbs** — *Cybersecurity Awareness in Higher Education*  
  - Identifies major knowledge gaps between perceived and actual student cybersecurity competence.
- **National Cybersecurity Alliance**  
  - Provides real-world data on student targeting patterns and practical prevention strategies.

All sources are cited using **MLA format** and documented in a formal **Works Cited** page.

---

## Proposed Solutions

### **1. Mandatory Cybersecurity Awareness Course**
A required first-semester course (similar to SDV 100) covering:
- Phishing and scam identification  
- Strong password practices  
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)  
- Device and account security  
- Safe network usage  

Research indicates that **early training produces long-term secure habits** and reduces incident frequency.

### **2. Continuous Cybersecurity Awareness Initiatives**
- Monthly newsletters  
- Scam and phishing alerts  
- Security update reminders  
- Interactive workshops  

These initiatives reinforce cybersecurity as a **shared responsibility**, not solely an IT department task.
