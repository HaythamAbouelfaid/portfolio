## Overview

**CupidSecure** is an AI-powered web platform that detects romance scams in real time by analyzing dating conversations and financial requests. It was built in ~30 hours at **HackFax × PatriotHacks 2026** and awarded **1st Place in the Blockchain, Cyber & Security track**.

Users can paste chat messages or upload screenshots from any dating app, receive an explainable risk score, and get concrete next steps *before* sending money. The platform also includes a Scammer Simulator for training and an enterprise-style dashboard that visualizes scam trends over time.

**Why this matters:**  
Romance scams cause over a billion dollars in reported losses every year, and many victims never come forward because of shame. CupidSecure is designed as a quiet, trusted **AI copilot** that helps people sanity-check conversations and understand red flags without needing to talk to anyone first.

## Key Features

### Conversation Analyzer (Text + Screenshots)

- Users can **paste conversations** or upload **screenshots** from dating and messaging apps.  
- A Gemini-powered analysis pipeline detects romance-scam tactics such as:
  - Love bombing and rapid escalation of intimacy.
  - Overseas deployment or remote work stories.
  - Crisis and emergency money requests.
  - Crypto, gift card, or wire transfer asks.
- The system returns:
  - A **0–100 risk score** with color-coded severity.
  - A **red flag timeline / heatmap** showing how risk increases across the conversation.
  - A list of **detected tactics** with short explanations written for non-technical users.

### Financial Request Check

- Dedicated module for when money comes up in a conversation.  
- Inputs:
  - Amount requested.
  - Payment method (e.g., bank transfer, gift cards, crypto).
  - Relationship duration (days/weeks).
  - Stated reason for the request.
- Combines these into a **financial risk score** with justification, flagging especially risky patterns like:
  - Large amounts early in the relationship.
  - High-risk payment methods.
  - Classic emergency narratives.
- Output is a simple recommendation like **“High risk – do not send”** plus a brief rationale.

### Practice Mode – Scammer Simulator

- Interactive **Scammer Simulator** where the AI plays various romance-scam personas:
  - Deployed soldier overseas.
  - Long-distance professional with sudden crisis.
  - “Investor” with guaranteed returns.
- Users chat with the AI as if it were a real person and try to spot red flags in real time.  
- After a set number of messages, the simulator:
  - Reveals the tactics it used.
  - Highlights missed warning signs.
  - Suggests better responses for next time.
- Designed for workshops, campus events, or self-guided training.

### Risk Dashboard

- An **enterprise-style dashboard** surfaces high-level metrics, including:
  - Active monitored users.
  - Risky conversations flagged in the last 7 days.
  - Estimated loss prevented.
  - Average risk score across the system.
- Visualizations:
  - **Weekly threat trend line**.
  - **Scam type distribution** (donut chart).
  - **Geographic hotspots** inside the US.
- Intended for future integrations with:
  - Dating platforms.
  - Counselors or victim-support organizations.
  - Campus safety offices.

### Embedded AI Assistant

- A persistent **Cupid AI assistant** appears across pages via a chat bubble.  
- Explains:
  - What each section does.
  - How to interpret risk scores and red flags.
  - What actions to take if a conversation looks unsafe.
- The goal is to make the product approachable for users who are anxious, non-technical, or unfamiliar with security tools.

## Design & UX

- **Theme:**  
  - Dark, cyber-security inspired aesthetic with magenta/purple accents to match the “Cupid” brand.
  - Subtle grid background and glow borders to create a **security dashboard** feel without overwhelming the user.

- **UX priorities:**
  - Clear information hierarchy:
    - Hero → Features → Analyzer → Practice → Dashboard → Mission → FAQ → Team.
  - Simple, high-contrast CTAs such as **“Analyze a Conversation Now”**.
  - Readable risk summaries and action lists rather than dense paragraphs.
  - Privacy-first messaging (in-memory analysis, no long-term storage) surfaced prominently.

- **Micro-interactions:**
  - Hover states on navigation, cards, and buttons.
  - Smooth scroll between sections.
  - Light elevation animations on cards to reinforce clickability.

## Challenges & Learnings

- **Designing for shame and fear:**  
  Many users are embarrassed to talk about potential scams. We had to design flows that feel private, non-judgmental, and safe to try in under a minute.

- **Explainability over raw scores:**  
  A single 0–100 number isn’t enough. It took iteration to combine:
  - A simple risk bar.
  - A red flag timeline.
  - Named tactics and recommended actions.
  into a layout that people can scan quickly.

- **Realistic but safe simulation:**  
  Building the Scammer Simulator required balancing realism with safety—capturing common scam narratives without veering into exploitative or overly graphic content.

- **Timeboxing an end-to-end product:**  
  Within a hackathon weekend, we had to scope aggressively:
  - Focus on a clean, cohesive MVP.
  - Reuse components across pages.
  - Keep the backend thin and delegate as much as possible to the LLM.
