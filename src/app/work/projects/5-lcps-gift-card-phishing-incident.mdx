## Overview

This project documents a **real-world phishing incident** that targeted my **Loudoun County Public Schools (LCPS)** email account and escalated into a **gift card scam via SMS** — a classic **Business Email Compromise (BEC)** attack pattern.

Instead of ignoring the attempt, I treated it as a **SOC-style incident investigation**, collecting evidence, performing **email header forensics**, analyzing **social engineering tactics**, and documenting the full **incident response lifecycle** aligned with industry frameworks.

**Why this matters:**  
Phishing and BEC attacks remain one of the **most common and costly threats** facing organizations today. This case study demonstrates how analysts investigate suspicious messages, validate sender authenticity, and prevent financial loss through **technical analysis, documentation, and awareness**.

---

## Incident Summary

- **Date:** October 22, 2025  
- **Target:** LCPS Substitute Teacher email account  
- **Initial Vector:** Email titled *“Weekly Operations Review”*  
- **Escalation:** SMS messages requesting urgent gift card purchases  
- **Objective:** Financial theft via social engineering  

The attacker impersonated an LCPS supervisor using a **generic Outlook.com email address**, requested confidentiality, and attempted to pressure immediate action — all hallmark indicators of a BEC gift card scam.

---

## Key Investigation Steps

### 1. Evidence Collection & Preservation
- Collected the original phishing email and follow-up SMS messages.
- Redacted sensitive personal information to prevent data exposure.
- Preserved original artifacts (email headers, screenshots) to maintain integrity and chain-of-custody.

### 2. Email Header Forensics
- Extracted full message headers from Microsoft Outlook.
- Analyzed:
  - **From** and **Return-Path** fields
  - **SPF, DKIM, and DMARC** authentication results
  - Received mail server chain
- Verified the email originated from an **external Outlook.com domain**, not LCPS infrastructure.

### 3. Automation & Scripting
- Built a custom Python script (`header_quickcheck.py`) to:
  - Parse raw email headers using regex
  - Extract SPF/DKIM/DMARC results
  - Highlight external domains and authentication mismatches
- Demonstrated how lightweight tooling can assist analysts during triage.

### 4. Social Engineering Analysis
Identified multiple manipulation techniques:
- **Authority:** Impersonation of a supervisor figure  
- **Urgency:** Pressure to act immediately  
- **Confidentiality:** Requests to avoid verification  
- **Financial Lure:** Gift card purchase request  

### 5. Incident Response & Reporting
- Did **not** engage further with the attacker.
- Reported the incident to LCPS IT security.
- Blocked the associated phone number and email address.
- Documented findings in a formal technical report and infographic.

## Key Takeaways

- Email authentication checks (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) must be paired with **contextual analysis** — passing checks alone do not guarantee legitimacy.
- BEC attacks rely heavily on **human trust and urgency**, not malware.
- Proper documentation and reporting are critical components of effective incident response.
- Turning real incidents into structured case studies accelerates hands-on cybersecurity learning.
